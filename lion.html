The LION

Characteristics

The lion is a well-muscled cat with a long body, large head, and short legs.
Size and appearance vary considerably between the sexes.
They have short, yellow-gold to dark brown fur, powerful forelegs, and a dark tuft at the end of their tail
Adult males possess a thick mane, which can protect their neck during fights and intimidate rivals.

Behaviour

Lions are the most social wild cats, living in family groups called prides (4–12 related females, their offspring, and 2–3, often unrelated, males).
They are apex predators that hunt in groups, primarily at night.
Known for their lethargy, they rest up to 20–21 hours daily to conserve energy.

Habitat

Lions primarily inhabit the savannas, grasslands, open woodlands, and thick bush scrub of sub-Saharan Africa, with a small, endangered population of Asiatic lions in India’s Gir Forest.
They are highly adaptable, avoiding dense forests and extreme deserts, preferring areas with sufficient cover for hunting and shade for resting.
